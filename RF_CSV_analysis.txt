Random Forest is perfectly suited for datasets with tabular statistical features like size of packets, time intervals, bursts, direction of packets. It works well with numerical and categorical data. Random Forest works better than Decision Tree as it handles small anf large feature sets well, it is resistant to noise and outliers which are present in case of Tor traffic, and scalable. Its main advantage is that it generates a random subset of features. 
To ensure higher accuracy, I used cross-validation with 5 folds. This method guarantees less dependence on random training/testing separation and gives a more stable final result.

Feature Importance graph for RF shows that only a subset of features is rather important in traffic classification for Tor website. Those features are the range of packet sizes, max size of burst and mean size of burst. Those features distinguish different categories of websites. The diagonal in confusion matrix is clearly expressed, but we can see that there are misses between similar websites.
Among all trained models RF showed the most precise result on a small dataset because it is suited for statistical dependencies and it does not require feature extension and high data volumes.
